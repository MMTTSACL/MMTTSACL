MMTTS: Visual-Guided Text-to-Speech with Hierarchical Multi-Modal Learning

Abstract

Abstract: Visual-guided text-to-speech (TTS) aims to synthesize audio that aligns with the content, pitch, rhythm, and other acoustic features, while also considering the physical environment to incorporate realistic reverberations. Recently,language modeling with multiple modalities has achieved impressive performance in many generative tasks. However, the long sequence length, and complex acoustic characteristics along the modality gap hinder its applications to visual-guided text-to-speech. In this work, we propose MMTTS, the first Multi-Modal TTS model with discrete acoustic units. The audios are tokenized by a pre-trained neural codec, in which multiple residual vector-quantizers (RVQs) are adopted. To address the challenges in multi-modal language modeling, we utilize a hierarchical transformer with acoustic modeling across RVQs and temporal modeling within RVQs. Furthermore, we propose multi-scale cross-modal matching, including text-visual and audio-visual fusion, to integrate visual information. To alleviate the data scarcity, we introduce efficient self-supervised learning to extend MMTTS to low-resource scenarios. Experimental results show that MMTTS achieves state-of-the-art performance, outperforming previous advanced approaches and cascaded systems by a large margin. Audio samples are available at http://MMTTSACL.github.io/
